<p><p>The TWS (True Wireless Stereo) and noise-cancelling headphone market features two main noise reduction techniques: Passive Noise Reduction and Active Noise Cancellation. Passive Noise Reduction relies on physical design elements, such as ear cushions, to block ambient sound. In contrast, Active Noise Cancellation uses advanced MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) technology to detect external noise and generate counteracting sound waves, effectively minimizing unwanted sound. Both methods enhance the listening experience, catering to varied consumer preferences for sound isolation and comfort.</p></p>
