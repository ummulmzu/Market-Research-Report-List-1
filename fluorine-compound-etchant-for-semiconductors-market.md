<p><p>The fluorine compound etchant market for semiconductors comprises various types, each serving specific functions in the microfabrication process. Hydrofluoric acid is primarily used for silicon wafer etching, effectively removing oxide layers. Nitrogen trifluoride acts as a cleaning agent, efficiently removing photoresist and other contaminants. Ammonium fluoride is utilized to etch silicon dioxide. Iodine pentafluoride is favored for its ability to etch various materials with precision. Other compounds include specialized formulations tailored for unique semiconductor applications, enhancing overall etching performance.</p></p>
