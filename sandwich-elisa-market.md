<p><p>Sandwich ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is a highly sensitive immunoassay technique used for the detection and quantification of specific proteins or antigens in a sample. This method involves the use of two antibodies: a capture antibody that binds to the target antigen on the microplate, and a detection antibody that is conjugated to an enzyme, which produces a measurable signal upon substrate addition. Sandwich ELISA is widely utilized in various fields such as clinical diagnostics, research, and drug development due to its accuracy and reliability.</p><p>The Sandwich ELISA market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of chronic and infectious diseases, along with the rising demand for point-of-care testing.
