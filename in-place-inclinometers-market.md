<p><p>In-Place Inclinometers are essential tools for measuring angles of inclination in various applications, primarily in geotechnical and structural monitoring. The market can be divided into two main types: vertical and level inclinometers. Vertical inclinometers are designed to measure vertical displacements and tilt, typically installed in boreholes for monitoring subsurface stability. Level inclinometers, on the other hand, assess lateral movements and tilting, often used in surface applications to monitor structures like dams, retaining walls, and slopes for deformation and stability.</p></p>
