<p><p>The Monkeypox vaccine market includes several types based on their technological platforms. DNA vaccines use genetically engineered DNA to provoke an immune response, while non-replicating viral vector vaccines deliver a harmless virus carrying monkeypox genes to stimulate immunity. Protein subunit vaccines consist of harmless pieces of the monkeypox virus, fostering immune recognition. RNA vaccines utilize messenger RNA to instruct cells to produce virus proteins, enhancing immunity. The "Others" category may include novel or experimental vaccine types not classified in the main categories.</p></p>
